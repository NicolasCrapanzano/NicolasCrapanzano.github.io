Markus persson Aka "Notch"

Creator of minecraft

Relevant information

  • 1979 - Born on Stockholm, Sweden.
  • 1986 - Started programming with a Commodore 128 computer at age 7.
  • 2004 - At age 18 he started working with Midasplayer, later known as King.com. Where he met a developer called Jakob Porsér and became friends.
  • March 2009 - Persson and Porsér started working on their own games some for events like game jams. This caused them some problems with their employers, Midasplayer, wich prompted Persson to leave the company and join jAlbum.
  • May 2009 - Notch spent one week building a prototype for a 3D cave simulator, based on another game called "Infiniminer" by Zachtronics. -The game consisted on a Lego-like playstyle, where everything was made out of cubes, wich you could remove and add.
    -The first release of the prototype was called "Minecraft" and was published privately on the TigIRC forums the 16 of may, and a public version known as classic was released the next day and started seeing some small success among the users of the forum.
  • 2011 - By April Persson estimated that Minecraft sold 800,000 copies, and made US$33 million in revenue.
    -On november of the same year it was held the first convention dedicated to the game, called Minecon in Las Vegas and sold out the 4,500 tickets that were available.
  • 2014 - On September he sold Mojang to Microsoft for $2.5 billion and left the company.
  • 2020 - On may for the 11th anniversary of the game Mojang announced that Minecraft had reached over 200 million copies sold across all platforms.
